Market Signals

Why this layer is needed now.

The market is already signaling a clear need for stronger controls over AI execution, agent autonomy, tool use, operational trust, and governance at the point where actions occur.

Observed Signals

The examples below do not claim that Certor™ is the only possible solution. They show that the industry is already encountering the exact category of problem that an authorization layer is designed to address.

Anthropic — Computer Use Risk Signal

Anthropic states that computer use is a beta feature with unique risks, and that those risks are heightened when interacting with the internet.

Anthropic — Agentic Misalignment Signal

Anthropic research highlights that models’ intentional actions can become a source of harm, including cases that are not dependent on adversarial prompting.

OpenAI — Operator Control Signal

OpenAI describes user control at critical points as a core safety principle for Operator, indicating that action-taking systems require explicit control boundaries.

OpenAI — Computer Use Security Signal

OpenAI’s computer-use guidance recommends isolated environments, human oversight for high-impact actions, and treating page content as untrusted input.

OWASP — Agentic Application Risk Signal

OWASP’s Top 10 for Agentic Applications identifies critical security risks facing autonomous and agentic AI systems, confirming a growing execution-layer threat model.

OWASP — Agentic Skills Execution Signal

OWASP’s Agentic Skills Top 10 explicitly describes skills as the execution layer that gives agents real-world impact across major platforms.

NIST — GenAI Risk Management Signal

NIST’s Generative AI Profile states that organizations face unique risks from generative AI and need structured actions for AI risk management.

Industry Direction

Across research, product safety pages, and security frameworks, the pattern is consistent: more capable AI systems require stronger control, clearer accountability, and better action-layer safeguards.

What These Signals Point To

These signals converge on a common structural need: the gap is no longer only model capability, but the absence of a distinct layer that decides whether execution is authorized before action proceeds.

Governance Need

Organizations increasingly need enforceable control, not only policy text or prompt-level restraint.

Operational Need

Agentic systems are gaining real-world reach through tools, browsers, skills, APIs, and autonomous workflows.

Architectural Need

The missing layer is authorization before execution — a distinct control boundary between intent and downstream action.

Positioning Note

Certor™ should be read in this context: not as a response to one single company or one single regulation, but as an architectural response to a broad and increasing category of execution-control problems.
