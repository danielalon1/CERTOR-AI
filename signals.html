Market Signals

Why this layer is needed now.

The market is already signaling a clear need for stronger controls over AI execution, agent autonomy, tool use, operational trust, and governance at the point where actions occur.

Observed Signals

The examples below show that the industry is already encountering the category of problem that an authorization layer is designed to address.

Anthropic — Computer Use Risk Signal

Anthropic describes computer use as a beta capability with heightened risks, especially when interacting with the internet.

Anthropic — Agentic Misalignment Signal

Anthropic research highlights cases where models’ intentional actions can become a source of harm.

OpenAI — Operator Control Signal

OpenAI describes user control at critical points as a core safety principle for action-taking systems.

OpenAI — Computer Use Security Signal

OpenAI guidance recommends isolated environments and human oversight for high-impact actions.

OWASP — Agentic Application Risk Signal

OWASP’s Top 10 for Agentic Applications identifies critical security risks for autonomous and agentic systems.

NIST — GenAI Risk Management Signal

NIST’s generative AI profile points to the need for structured action in AI risk management.

Positioning Note

Certor™ should be read in this context: not as a response to one single company or one single regulation, but as an architectural response to a broad and increasing category of execution-control problems.
