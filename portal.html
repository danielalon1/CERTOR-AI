Core Thesis

The unresolved question in advanced AI systems is no longer only what they can generate, but what they are allowed to execute. Certor™ is positioned at that exact boundary: authorization before execution.

Why This Matters

Across research, enterprise deployment, live tools, and agent security failures, the same gap keeps appearing: systems gain capability faster than they gain control.

Capability Is Rising

Models are becoming stronger, cheaper, and more widely integrated across workflows and products.

Execution Is Expanding

AI systems are moving beyond generation into tools, APIs, browsers, and action-taking environments.

Control Is Missing

The missing layer is a decision boundary that determines whether execution should proceed at all.

Execution Flow

Certor™ is designed to sit between intent and action, introducing a control step before execution is permitted.

1. Request

A model, user, or agent produces intent directed toward an action.

2. Authorization

Certor evaluates whether execution should proceed under explicit control.

3. Execution

Downstream action proceeds only after authorization is satisfied.
