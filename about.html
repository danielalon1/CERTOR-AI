About Certor™

An authorization architecture for AI execution.

Certor™ is an architecture initiative focused on authorization at the execution layer of AI systems. Its central premise is that execution should be subject to an independent authorization boundary, rather than relying solely on model output or application logic.

Architectural Premise

Certor™ is built around a structural distinction between generation and authorization: models may generate outputs, but execution requires an additional, independent decision layer.

Separate Layer

Certor is designed as a distinct layer positioned before execution, rather than as an instruction embedded inside model behavior.

Execution Focus

The architecture focuses on whether requested actions should proceed, be deferred, or be blocked at the execution boundary.

Portable Design

The design direction is model-agnostic and intended for deployment across varied agent, tool, and runtime environments.

Research Direction

The Certor research line progresses through three layers: foundational framing of the authorization problem, architectural specification of the control layer, and MVP-level validation at the execution boundary.

Research Line

Concept → Architecture → Validation

Operational Direction

Development is oriented toward real authorization workflows, execution-bound control, traceable decision paths, and model-agnostic deployment options.

Current Orientation

The active path emphasizes authority-gated execution, permit-based action control, and operational validation through MVP infrastructure.
