About Certor™

Certor™ is an architecture initiative focused on authorization at the execution layer of AI systems. Its central premise is that execution should be subject to an independent authorization boundary, rather than relying solely on model output or application logic.

Architectural Focus

Certor is designed as a separate layer positioned before execution. Its purpose is to evaluate whether a requested action should proceed, be deferred, or be blocked at the point where execution would otherwise occur.

Design Principle

The project is built around a structural distinction between generation and authorization: models may generate outputs, but execution requires an additional, independent decision layer.

Research Direction

The Certor research line progresses through three layers: foundational framing of the authorization problem, architectural specification of the control layer, and MVP-level validation at the execution boundary.

Operational Direction

Development is oriented toward real authorization workflows, execution-bound control, traceable decision paths, and model-agnostic deployment options.

Intellectual Property

Certor™ and its underlying architectural concepts are part of an ongoing intellectual property and patent development process.

Related intellectual property is owned by Daniel Alon, originator of the Certor architecture and the “Authority Before Execution” model.
